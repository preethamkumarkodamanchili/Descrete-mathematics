Discrete Mathematics

Description

Discrete mathematics is the study of mathematical structures that are fundamentally discrete rather than continuous. In contrast to real numbers that have the property of varying "smoothly", the objects studied in discrete mathematics – such as integers, graphs, and statements in logic– do not vary smoothly in this way, but have distinct, separated values. Discrete mathematics is the branch of mathematics dealing with objects that can assume only distinct, separated values. ... Whereas discrete objects can often be characterized by integers, continuous objects require real numbers.
